Malay Food

Nasi Lemak

Nasi Lemak is a Malay fragrant rice dish cooked in coconut milk and pandan leaf. It is considered as Malaysia national dish.

Asam Pedas

Asam pedas is a Minangkabau and Malay sour and spicy fish stew dish. In Malaysia, it is most popular in Melaka.

Nasi Kerabu

Nasi Kerabu is a Malay rice dish, a type of nasi ulam, in which blue-coloured rice is eaten with dried fish or fried chicken, crackers, pickles and other salads.

Nasi Dagang

Nasi Dagang is a Malaysian dish consisting of rice steamed in coconut milk, fish curry and extra ingrediants such as pickled cucumber and carrots.

Patin Tempoyak

Tempoyak or also known as fermented durian, is a traditional condiment in Malaysia often mixed with coconut milk curries or pounded with chilies into a spicy dip.

Mi Bandung

Mi Bandung is a traditional cuisine originated from Muar,Johor. The word Bandung in its name derives from the literal meaning of the word in Malay languange which is mixed.

Curry Puff

Keria

Onde Onde

A traditional green-coloured balls of rice cake filled with liquid palm sugar and coated in grated coconut.

Kuih Sagu Merah

Made of pearl sagu and sugar, boiled till translucent and cooked, cooled in tray, then, cut to desired pieces and rolled in salted fresh grated coconut.

Ketayap

A cylindrical shaped kuih with caramalised grated coconut flesh inside and a green pancake skin wrapping it.

Keropok Lekor

Keropok Lekor is a traditional Malay fish cracker snack originating from the state of Terengganu. It is made from fish and sago flour and seasoned with salt and sugar.
